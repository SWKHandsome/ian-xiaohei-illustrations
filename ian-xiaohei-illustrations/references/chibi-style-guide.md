# Chibi Style Guide

## 1) Definition and project purpose

Chibi style in this project is a **compact character-first icon language** used for profile/avatar-scale visuals, quick emotional states, and lightweight identity variants.

Target direction:
- Soft painterly anime feeling
- Semi-watercolor texture with visible brush marks
- Relaxed and readable at small size
- Original designs only (no copying existing IP/artwork)

Use chibi assets when the task needs:
- Avatar/icon output
- Character mood variants
- Friendly identity marker that complements, not replaces, main Xiaohei article illustrations

---

## 2) Proportion guidelines (chibi-specific)

Recommended proportion ranges:
- Head-to-body ratio: **1.8:1 to 2.5:1** (head visually dominant)
- Eye area width: **~30%-42% of face width**
- Facial feature clustering: keep features centered; avoid realistic spacing
- Limbs/hands: simplified and short; avoid anatomical realism

Silhouette rules:
- Keep one clean, recognizable outer silhouette
- Avoid dense accessories that break readability under 128px
- Primary read should survive at 64px icon size

---

## 3) Expression and emotion guidelines

Default expression for this character line:
- Relaxed / slightly sleepy
- Half-lidded eyes
- Gentle, low-contrast emotion (not exaggerated meme expressions)

Expression control:
- Eyes drive ~70% of emotion signal
- Mouth should stay minimal (small curve, short line, or tiny shape)
- Keep brows/hair shadows soft; avoid harsh dramatic contrast

Emotion variants to support:
- Calm focus
- Soft confusion
- Quiet confidence
- Light curiosity

---

## 4) Pose and action library

Core icon-safe pose set:
1. Neutral front-facing bust
2. Slight 3/4 turn with relaxed shoulders
3. Head tilt + tiny hand gesture
4. Float pose with minimal body hint
5. Listening pose with tech accessory emphasis

Action micro-library (for small icon variants):
- Blink/half-blink
- Tiny nod
- Soft hover
- Small symbol interaction (dot, spark, ring, plus)

Pose constraints:
- Center composition in square canvas
- Keep action readable without text labels
- Do not overload with scene/background objects

---

## 5) Color palette specification (chibi assets)

Preferred palette family: pastel, muted, cool tones.

Base palette (guideline):
- Soft Blue: `#AFCFE6`
- Mist Teal: `#9CCFD3`
- Muted Purple: `#B8AAD8`
- Dust Indigo: `#8FA8C9`
- Highlight Ice: `#EAF4FF`

Usage:
- One dominant cool tone + one support tone + one accent tone
- Keep saturation moderate/low
- Highlights soft and diffused; no hard specular edges
- Shadows should be gentle and painterly, not sharp cel-shading

---

## 6) Do / Don’t (softness + originality)

### Do
- Keep brush texture visible and organic
- Use layered soft edges instead of vector-clean cutouts
- Keep character simple and memorable at small sizes
- Design accessories (headphone/headpiece) as original abstract forms
- Maintain a calm, gentle visual rhythm

### Don’t
- Don’t imitate named artists, known franchises, or existing mascots
- Don’t use product-identical hardware shapes/logos
- Don’t over-render details that disappear at icon scale
- Don’t use noisy backgrounds, heavy gradients, or hard black shadows
- Don’t convert style into flat infographic/vector icon look

---

## 7) Relationship to main Xiaohei illustration style

How chibi differs:
- Chibi: character-centric, compact, avatar-ready, painterly softness
- Main Xiaohei style: 16:9 whiteboard-like conceptual article illustration with structural storytelling

How they complement each other:
- Shared originality rules and clarity goals
- Chibi provides identity/emotion anchors
- Main Xiaohei visuals carry article-level concept explanation
- Both should stay minimal, readable, and avoid derivative imitation
